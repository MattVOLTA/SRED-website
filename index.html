Why it exists

Built for the founder who cannot afford a year-end archaeology project

Early teams know the hard technical work when it happens. By filing season, the source links, eval failures, tradeoffs, and approvals are scattered. This repo gives the team a small operating rhythm before that context disappears.

During the sprint

Capture as it happens

Technical uncertainty, experiments, failures, model evals, decisions, and the evidence that proves when they happened.

During the month

Review what holds up

What is accepted, excluded, weak, blocked, sensitive, or waiting on finance, privacy, CTO, or advisor input.

Before advisor review

Hand over a clean binder

Source-backed narratives, cost support, privacy decisions, approval status, and the unresolved questions.

What it produces

A source-linked workspace that can survive scrutiny

Each artifact answers a question the claim process will eventually ask. Facts, interpretation, approvals, and sensitive raw material stay in separate lanes.

Control board

Claim question index

The founder control board for what is answered, blocked, excluded, or waiting for human review. One place to see where the whole claim stands.

Evidence index and cards

Dated artifacts from Git, tickets, evals, docs, meetings, logs, finance, and payroll stay traceable.

Experiment ledger

Hypotheses, attempts, tests, results, and conclusions tied to the technical unknown being investigated.

AI eval register

Models, prompts, datasets, baselines, thresholds, metrics, traces, and failure patterns captured while fresh.

Time and finance ledgers

People, contractors, cloud, model API, grants, assets, and locations mapped to source documents.

Final review binder

An advisor-facing index of approved facts, draft narratives, evidence, costs, privacy status, and open questions.

Useful evidence support, not tax advice

This operating system helps a startup preserve what happened and prepare materials for qualified review.

It does not decide final eligibility, calculate a credit, promise a refund, file a claim, or replace a CPA or SR&ED specialist.
Technical leadApproves technical facts and experiment conclusions.
Finance ownerApproves source financial records and effort inputs.

AdvisorReviews SR&ED treatment, forms, expenditures, and open questions.
